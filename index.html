Who we are

A parent-led voice for public schools

Community Education Councils began in 2004, replacing the old community school boards. The council advises the Chancellor and the Panel on Educational Policy on the needs of the district, and advocates for District 26 parents and schools.

There are 32 Community Education Councils in New York City — one for each school district. Parents hold the majority of seats, with students and community members in the rest.

What the council does
  • Promotes high educational standards for student learning across the district
  • Works with School Leadership Teams and supports them where it can
  • Advises the Chancellor and the Panel on Educational Policy on district concerns
  • Reviews the district's educational programs and their impact on students
  • Approves the school zoning lines for District 26

The council and how it works
